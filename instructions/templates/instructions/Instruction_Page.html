Introduction

Welcome and thank you for taking part in this Economics Experiment. This experiment will last for around 1 hour. If you read the instructions carefully, you can earn a considerable amount of money depending on your decisions and chance. Your earnings will be paid out to you in cash (Venmo) at the end of the experiment.

Before we begin, we ask that you turn off your cell phones for the duration of this experiment. We also ask that you do not communicate with anyone for the duration of this experiment and only use the software provided to you on your devices. Failure to comply with these rules may result in dismissal from this experiment and you will not be paid any earnings you may have otherwise received.

Overview

This experiment consists of 21 rounds of repeated tasks administered in three 7-round blocks. You will be compensated based on a randomly selected belief you reported across all rounds.

Rounds

In each round, you will be assigned a color: Red or Blue. Each color is equally likely to be assigned for a round (50-50 chance). Note that the color in each round will be chosen independently of the colors in other rounds.

Your task is to guess the chance that the color assigned to you is Red. In particular, for each round you will make two guesses for the question:

What do you think is the chance (0-100)% that the color assigned for the round is Red?

You are given 4 signals to help you guess. These signals will come from four distinct robots (more on that later).

The timeline for each set of guesses in a round is as follows

  1. You will receive 3 signals.
  2. Then, you will make your first guess (Guess 1) about the chance that the color assigned to you is red based on those 3 signals.
  3. You will receive a 4th signal.
  4. Then, you will make your second guess (Guess 2) about the chance that the color assigned to you is Red based on all the 4 signals given.

Signals

Signals before Guess 1

Before making your first guess (Guess 1), you will be shown three signals generated by Robots X, Y, and Z (one signal each). Each robot’s signal correctly states the assigned color of the round with 55% chance.

For example, if the color assigned for the round is Red, then Robot X will state the color Red with 55% chance and Blue with 45% chance. Robots Y and Z will do the same.

Signals after Guess 1

Before making your second guess (Guess 2), you will receive a 4th signal. This 4th signal can come from Robot Z with 50% chance or from Robot W with 50% chance. If it is from Robot Z, it will repeat the signal it produced before Guess 1. If it is from Robot W, then the 4th signal will match the assigned color for the round with 99% chance. You will not know whether the 4th signal was generated by Robot Z or Robot W.

For example, if the assigned color is Red then Robot W will state the color Red with 99% chance and Blue with 1% chance. The 4th signal repeats Robot Z’s signal with 50% chance or reports Robot W’s color with 50% chance.

Blocks

There will be three blocks with 7 rounds per block. As you go through each block, more information is presented. That is, a summary table of 3000 simulated assigned colors and signal draws will be presented with the four signals in a round. Note that your task remains the same. You will still make two guesses about the chance that the assigned color for the round is red--one before receiving the 4th signals and one after receiving it.

In Block 1, no summary table will be presented with your 4 signals. For Blocks 2 and 3, there will be further instructions for the summary tables to be presented.

Note that your task remains the same for all blocks: make two guesses about the chance that the assigned color for the round is red.

Summary


  1. This experiment will have 21 rounds of belief updating tasks divided into three 7-round blocks.
  2. For each round of a belief updating task, a color will be randomly assigned that is either Red or Blue (with equal probability). In each round, we ask you to make two guesses about the chance that the assigned color for the round is red.
  3. In Block 1, you will be given 3 signals then you will make your first guess (Guess 1). Then, you will be given a 4th signal after which you make your second guess (Guess 2).
  4. In Block 2, will be given a summary table of assigned colors and the first three signals from a large number of simulated rounds. This summary table will be presented along with your first three signals for the round.
  5. In Block 3, the summary table from Block 2 will be shown as well. In addition to this, you will be given a second summary table of assigned colors and the 3rd and 4th signal from the same large number of simulated rounds, which will be restricted based on the 1st and 2nd signals you received for the round. This table is presented along with the 4th signal.

Payment


Your payment will depend on two randomly selected guesses among all your guesses across the 60 rounds: one is selected among all your first guesses for each round (Guess 1) and the other is selected among all your second guesses for each round (Guess 2).

The guess will be used to fill up the following list of 100 questions:

Question #n Which would you choose:

  • Option A win $10 if assigned color is Red
  • Option B win $10 with n% chance

where Question #n goes from 1 to 100.

For example, suppose that you stated that there is 75% chance that the assigned color is Red from the selected Guess 1. Then, we will fill up the list of questions for your by choosing Option A for all questions from Q1 to Q74 and Option B for all question from Q75 onwards.

We will randomly choose one of the questions (with equal chance) and pay you according to the answer for that question. Notice that there is no incentive to lie about the switch point Qx since you may get an option that you prefer less.

This is similarly done for the payment from randomly selected Guess 2. Your total earnings at the end of the experiment is calculated as follows:

Your total earnings = $2 show-up fee + earnings from a randomly selected Guess 1 + earnings from a randomly selected Guess 2.

Note: The payment scheme is designed in a way to ensure that the best way to maximize your earnings is to report your true guess. For example, for guesses about the chance of your assigned color being red, you maximize your earnings by reporting your true beliefs for all rounds.
